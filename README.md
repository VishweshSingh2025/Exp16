AIM

To study and implement Natural Language Processing (NLP) techniques in Python using the NLTK library for text preprocessing, tokenization, stop word removal, stemming, lemmatization, part-of-speech tagging, and frequency analysis.

_____________________________________________________________________________________________________

THEORY

Natural Language Processing (NLP) is a field of artificial intelligence that focuses on enabling computers to understand, interpret, and process human language. Text data often requires preprocessing before analysis, which includes breaking text into smaller units (tokenization), removing unnecessary words (stop words), reducing words to their root form (stemming and lemmatization), identifying grammatical roles (POS tagging), and analyzing word frequency. The NLTK (Natural Language Toolkit) library in Python provides various tools and datasets to perform these operations efficiently, making it widely used for text mining, sentiment analysis, and language modeling.

ONE-LINE EXPLANATIONS OF FUNCTIONS

nltk.download() – Downloads required NLP datasets and resources.

word_tokenize() – Splits text into individual words or tokens.

sent_tokenize() – Splits text into sentences.

stopwords.words() – Returns a list of commonly used words to be removed.

PorterStemmer() – Reduces words to their root form using stemming.

stem() – Converts a word to its stem form.

WordNetLemmatizer() – Converts words to their dictionary base form.

lemmatize() – Returns the meaningful base form of a word.

pos_tag() – Assigns grammatical tags (noun, verb, etc.) to each word.

FreqDist() – Calculates frequency distribution of words in text.

most_common() – Returns the most frequent words with their counts.
